Email Engine - 06

Cold stays cold.
Warm goes to Klaviyo.

Why Klaviyo is not used for cold outreachKlaviyo uses shared IP infrastructure and prohibits sending to contacts who have not engaged. Using it for cold B2B outreach lands every email in spam and risks account suspension. Smartlead handles cold. Klaviyo handles warm. The two platforms serve two distinct audiences and must not be mixed.

Five triggers that move a contact from Smartlead to Klaviyo

Assessment booked
Prospect replied and booked via Calendly. GHL tags "Assessment Booked." Zapier adds to Klaviyo "Warm Active" list and enrolls in Flow 1: confirmation, reminder, post-meeting follow-up.
Assessment attended
Client marks meeting outcome in GHL. Contact enters Klaviyo Flow 2: proposal summary, objection-address email, urgency touch, final check-in over 14 days.
Day 14 no reply
Smartlead sequence complete. Zapier adds to Klaviyo "Monthly Nurture" list. One real compliance news email per month for 6 months. Any Calendly click re-routes to Flow 1 automatically.
Closed won
Client signed. Enters Klaviyo Flow 4: 30/60/90-day check-ins, Month 3 upsell intro, Month 6 referral ask, Month 12 contract renewal prompt.
Closed lost
Enters Klaviyo Flow 5: 90-day pause then 3-email re-qualification. "A business near you just had a compliance audit triggered by a competitor complaint." Re-qualifies on any engagement.
GHL to Klaviyo wiring (Zapier)No native GHL-Klaviyo integration exists. Zapier is the bridge. One Zap per trigger type. All built during week 2. GHL tag applied triggers Zapier, which creates or updates the Klaviyo profile and adds the contact to the corresponding flow list. Data passed: name, email, business name, vertical, pipeline stage, risk level, number of compliance gaps found. Klaviyo uses these fields to personalize every flow email dynamically.

Sales Playbooks - 08

Be a human first.
Then be a salesperson.

The operator calls because they have information that genuinely protects the prospect. That is true. Act like it. Operational framing beats regulatory framing by 23% in connect rate (Gong.io).

Gatekeeper script

// Operational framing, not regulatory. 23% higher connect rate (Gong.io).

"Hi, quick question. I am trying to reach whoever handles technology and cybersecurity at the dealership. Is that the GM or the owner directly?"
If asked what it is about: "We work with South Florida dealerships on FTC data security compliance. There is a federal rule that changed in 2023. Fines run up to $53,000 per violation, per day. I want to make sure the right person has that information. Can I get them for 90 seconds?"
If blocked, "We have IT handled": "That is great, and this is different from IT support. It is a specific federal compliance requirement and most dealers with IT in place still have 2-3 documentation gaps. Can I speak to the owner for two minutes?"
If fully blocked, get email: "Could I get the best email for the GM or owner? I will send a 2-page FTC checklist they can review on their own time." Log in GHL immediately. Add contact to Smartlead Day 1 sequence via Apollo import if not already in system.

// "Per day" is the pivot. Named local peer is 2x more motivating than abstract statistic. Let the number land before continuing.

// Pause. Identity first, pitch second.

"Reason I am calling: we just wrapped an assessment for a dealership in [nearby WPB area - pulled from Apollo geography] and they had three compliance gaps they did not know about. One would have triggered an FTC fine of up to $53,000 per day for ongoing non-compliance."

// "Per day." Let it land. Two seconds of silence is correct.
